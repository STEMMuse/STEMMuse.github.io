Our story

Our journey from being graduate students in STEM to building a student organization and community for women in STEM, to founding a mentorship program and launching STEM Muse

As PhD students at at the University of Texas at Austin and women in STEM, Sophie Sanchez and Damla Cinoglu realized that students were isolated in their degree programs and unaware of career options outside of medicine and academia, experiencing little exposure to industry careers and few opportunities to engage meaningfully with prospective employers.

Program development at the Society for Advancing Gender Equity in STEM (SAGES) at UT

To address these challenges, Sophie founded a UT organization (SAGES) which now has over 750 members and an advisory council of more than 20 UT faculty and staff.

When Damla joined the organization in 2019, she and Sophie launched a mentorship program to provide sustained, individualized guidance for women undergrad and grad students in STEM fields at UT and opportunities for them to explore industry careers. The program has blossomed to serve over 200 participants in its 3rd cycle in 2022. Inspired by success stories including mentees landing jobs & internships, we launched STEM Muse to extend our impact & scale beyond UT.

Transitioning to STEM Muse

In April of 2022, Sophie and Damla decided to transition from developing and running the SAGES mentorship program to founding STEM Muse to scale the community and the Mentorship program nationally.
