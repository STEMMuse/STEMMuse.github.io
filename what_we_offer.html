What we offer

Individualized, identity-conscious professional development and career support through a mentorship program and professional network for women in STEM

Women are underrepresented not only in US higher education but also in STEM careers. There is a retention problem in higher education with one study finding that 12% of non-mentored engineers left their major after one year. In STEM careers, women make up just 28% of the workforce and often do not rise to managerial roles. There is an even larger retention problem.

Our solution is a virtual community and a mentorship program with in-person programming that cultivate a network, create career opportunities, and foster a sense of belonging for women in STEM.

Our program is designed to meet our mentees’ needs, which starts with letting mentees select their mentors. We optimize that process with an algorithm so that as many get their top choice as possible. Our program matches academic mentees who are undergraduate students, graduate students, postdoctoral associates, and research staff with academic and industry mentors and enables flexible scheduling among mentorship pairs. We provide structure with group programming and monthly themed conversation guides to facilitate productive conversations in their mentorship meetings.

In 2022, we served a diverse group of mentees and mentors, including mentees from numerous STEM backgrounds, many of whom are from untraditional and underrepresented backgrounds.

Our community and tailored mentorship program address three pillars of recruitment, retention, and success.

We have channeled mentees into internships and full-time positions at our mentors’ companies. Our mentee driven program structure led to 88% of our participants reporting a very positive experience. We expect that sustained interactions and exposure to companies will improve assimilation of new employees– Our program helps mentees gain a better understanding of academic and professional roles and the skills they need to acquire to succeed in them, and they will have a support system in place before they even step into their offices on Day 1.

From UT Austin to beyond: What Starts Here Changes the World

We will be launching the STEM Muse pilot program in February 2023 in collaboration with the University of Texas at Austin’s Society for Advancing Gender Equity in STEM. Ultimately, our vision for STEM Muse is to be a national community of women in STEM supporting one another through flexible yet structured mentorship.

We take pride in our program with:

A flexible structure

Our program occurs via hybrid events, allowing both in-person and virtual interactions. Pairs meet for 6 months at their convenience (at least once per month via the format of their choosing).

Mentee-centric, algorithm-optimized matching

We use our mentees' ranking of mentor profiles to drive the matching process. Our algorithm enables as many mentees to be paired with their top choice mentor as possible.

Structured professional support

We provide structure to the mentorship interactions with frequent professional and networking events, resume-boosting workshops, a job board, and monthly conversation guides to facilitate engaging, productive conversations. The program is offered twice a year with numerous, allowing mentors and mentees enough time to get to know one another well, but also to connect with numerous other program participants in the course of a year.
